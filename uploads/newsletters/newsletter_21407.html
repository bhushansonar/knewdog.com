PSA-Dongfeng venture breaks ground for fourth plant
Automotive News China
Dongfeng Peugeot Citroen Automobile Co. began construction of its fourth assembly plant -- a factory intended to serve China's western regions. PSA Peugeot Citroen's joint venture with Dongfeng Motor Corp. will launch production at the plant in 2016. The 12.3 billion yuan ($2 billion) facility will build up to 360,000 vehicles a year, according to Dongfeng.

GM's Wuling plans new assembly plant in Guangxi
Automotive News China
SAIC-GM-Wuling Automobile Co. has signed an agreement with the government of southwest China's Guangxi province to build an assembly plant for its Baojun car brand, according to local media. General Motors' joint venture will spend 8 billion yuan ($1.3 billion) to build the facility in the company's hometown of Liuzhou, reported Guangxi Daily, a daily newspaper owned by the provincial government.

Tesla Model S sales debut on Alibaba online mall
Bloomberg
SHANGHAI -- Tesla Motors Inc. began taking online orders for the Model S electric car in China, joining General Motors and Volkswagen AG in selling vehicles through Alibaba Group Holding's online shopping mall. Tesla began deliveries of the Model S in the world's largest auto market in April. The automaker is seeking to cut down the time required to ramp up its sales by selling directly through the web rather than set up a network of dealerships across the country.

EV, plug-in hybrid sales triple in first 9 months
Automotive News China
Sales of electric vehicles and plug-in hybrids in China nearly tripled in the first nine months of the year, thanks to generous government incentives, the China Association of Automobile Manufacturers said. Automakers in China sold 38,163 units, including 22,258 EVs and 15,905 plug-in hybrids.

VW recalls 580,000 cars for rear axle glitch
Reuters
Volkswagen told China's quality watchdog that it planned to recall more than 580,000 cars in the country, after the agency launched an investigation in August. Volkswagen's joint venture in China, FAW-Volkswagen Automotive Co., will recall 563,605 New Sagitars produced between May 2011 and May 2014 due to a problem with the rear axle arm of the cars, the General Administration of Quality Supervision, Inspection and Quarantine said in a statement on its website.
